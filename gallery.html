Gallery

Clock Tower (Ghanta Ghar)

The Clock Tower, locally known as Ghanta Ghar, is a prominent landmark in Jodhpur built by Maharaja Sardar Singh. This iconic structure stands in the heart of the city's bustling market area and serves as a gathering point for locals and tourists alike.

Umaid Bhawan Palace

Umaid Bhawan Palace is one of the largest private residences in the world, part of which is managed by Taj Hotels. Built between 1929 and 1943, this magnificent palace blends eastern and western architectural influences and is named after Maharaja Umaid Singh.

Mandore Gardens

Mandore Gardens, the ancient capital of Marwar, features beautiful cenotaphs (dewals) of Jodhpur's rulers. These architectural marvels are built in the style of Hindu temples instead of the typical chattri-style cenotaphs found elsewhere in Rajasthan.

Rajasthan High Court

The Rajasthan High Court in Jodhpur is housed in a beautiful red sandstone building that features impressive architecture. Established in 1949, it's one of the oldest high courts in India and continues to serve as an important judicial institution.

Mehrangarh Fort Path

The winding path leading to Mehrangarh Fort offers stunning views of the blue city. Built around 1460 by Rao Jodha, the fort stands 410 feet above the city and is enclosed by impressive thick walls. The path itself is a journey through Jodhpur's history.

Jaswant Thada

Jaswant Thada is a cenotaph built in 1899 in memory of Maharaja Jaswant Singh II. This architectural landmark is made of intricately carved white marble and sits above a small lake, creating a peaceful retreat away from the bustling city.
